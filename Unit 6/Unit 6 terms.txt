Fiscal Period: The length of time for which a business summarizes its finacial 
information and reports its financial performance

Fiscal Year: A fiscal period consisting of twelve consecutive months.

Work Sheet: A columnar accounting form used to summarize the general ledger information
needed to prepare financial statements.

Balance Sheet: A financial statement that reports the value of a business's assets,
liabilities, and owner's equity on a specific date is called a balance sheet.

Income statement: A financial statement showing the revenue and expenses for a
fiscal period.

Net income: the diffrences between total revenue and total expenses when total 
revenue is greater.

Net loss: The diffrenence between total revenue and total expenses when total 
expenses are greater.

Adjusting Entries: Journal Entries recorded to update general ledger accounts 
at the end of a fiscal period.
